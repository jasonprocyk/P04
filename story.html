I took a few different paths to become an Interaction Researcher

As far back as I can remember, I always wanted to be a paleontologist. Wait...So my five-year-old self hadn't quite figured it out yet. That's okay. I have been making things with computers for a while. I began to discover design as a discipline much later, and I dove into the wonderful world of qualitative research a short time after that.

Digital Tinkering

I love to make, model, simulate, iterate, evaluate, and remake. I learned (or rather "started learning") at a young age to recruit computers in this cause. Even away from work and away from school, I consider myself a digital tinkerer.

A Different Direction

There was a time where I put down technology to see what I could find in literature and philosophy. I completed a Bachelor of Arts in Humanities, and managed to spend a semester in Greece along the way.

I value the cross experience, and I feel it is especially useful for more conceptual projects and design fictions. I learned to engage with ideas at a high level and to draw on the wisdom of great thinkers.

Discovering Design

I went to the School of Interactive Arts and Technology at SFU to learn design as a discipline. I learned and practiced user-centered design methods, and I began to focus on interactive systems and web technologies.

Diving Into Research

In the summer before the final year of my undergraduate studies, I earned a grant from NSERC to do interaction design research for one of my professors. It started simply as a curiosity—something new and unusual but also more focused and more challenging—and turned into much more.

That summer project yielded a year-long investigation into location-based storytelling that produced a fully functional web application, a pair of conference paper submissions (with a trip to CHI 2013 in Paris), and further funding for graduate studies.
