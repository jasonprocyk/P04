I mix experimental designs with qualitative research to make new and unusual interactive systems

Take my latest research project, GEMS. I wanted to explore how people can use location as a context for reflecting on and documenting meaningful personal experiences. I designed, developed, and evaluated a location-based storytelling game that challenges players to record memories and store them in real places for either future generations or present contacts to find.

Over the course of the project, I put together a fully-functional, Node.js web application, published a short work-in-progress paper (in the proceedings of CHI 2013), conducted two three-week user studies with a total of 15 participants, and wrote a full paper describing the design of the system and the findings of the study (in submission to CSCW 2014).
